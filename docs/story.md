# Story

## Premise

Helix Corporation is developing experimental temporal-control hardware for military deployment. Its most advanced prototype, the **Chronos Core**, is held inside the deepest vault of the **Helix Temporal Research Facility**.

The player is a professional infiltrator who specializes in stealing time technology. Their **Chrono Recall** device is an unstable derivative of the same research: it can restore a short span of recent reality, but only a few times before its temporal cells are exhausted.

An anonymous client wants the Core removed intact. The operation is theft, not sabotage. Destroying the facility, fighting its security force, or exposing the client is outside the contract.

## Operation: Black Minute

**English:** OPERATION: BLACK MINUTE

**한국어:** 작전명: 검은 1분

**Facility:** HELIX TEMPORAL RESEARCH FACILITY / 헬릭스 시간 연구시설

**Target:** CHRONOS CORE / 크로노스 코어

The plan is direct:

1. enter through the external service yard;
2. acquire physical access credentials;
3. neutralize or evade surveillance;
4. shut down the vault laser network;
5. obtain a temporary vault authorization;
6. remove the Chronos Core;
7. return to the original extraction yard.

## Delivery style

Story is communicated through the briefing, objective text, short terminal feedback, room names, and security-state changes. The MVP deliberately avoids long cinematics and dialogue trees.

The player should understand three facts before gaining control:

- what must be stolen;
- roughly where it is held;
- which security layers stand between infiltration and extraction.

## Tone

- precise professional operation rather than superhero combat;
- hostile corporate research environment rather than a ruined dungeon;
- limited, costly temporal technology rather than consequence-free magic;
- a clean theft whose best outcome is that Helix never understands the full route.

## Current narrative limits

There is one operation, no campaign save, no voiced radio dialogue, no branching client choice, and no post-mission cinematic. These are intentional MVP limits, not implied completed features.
